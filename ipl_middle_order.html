Elite quadrant
Dhoni, AB de Villiers, Suryakumar Yadav, Shreyas Iyer and Tilak Varma all sit top-right — the only batsmen combining a 37+ average with a 140+ strike rate. Klaasen (avg 44, SR 163) is arguably the most destructive of the group despite fewer innings.
The lone anchor
JP Duminy occupies the top-left quadrant almost alone — an exceptional average of 51.8 but a conservative SR of 127. A classical accumulator in a format that rarely rewards them, yet he sustained it across 43 innings.
Explosive finishers
Rinku Patidar (SR 170), Pooran (SR 156) and Maxwell (SR 152) are the bottom-right cluster — high risk, high reward. Patidar's SR is the highest of any player with 30+ innings, making him one of the most feared death-over hitters in the competition.

Average — the story
Duminy leads all qualifiers at 51.8, a remarkable figure for a T20 middle-order bat. Dhoni (42.7) and Klaasen (44.2) follow — both known for finishing games, not just surviving. Iyer (43.7) is the standout Indian batsman in this metric, suggesting a consistency often underappreciated in the debate around India's number 4.
Volume vs efficiency
Karthik (136 innings, avg 26.9) and Yuvraj (106 innings, avg 26.8) illustrate how longevity and volume can mask modest averages. Compare them to Klaasen (42 innings, avg 44.2) — a player who came in later but made every knock count far more consistently.
The Indian contingent
Among Indians with 50+ innings at 4/5, Dhoni stands alone at the top. Pant (34.1) and Rayudu (28.7) are the next most prolific, though Tilak Varma — still early in his career — already averages 40.8 across 55 innings and looks like a future mainstay.

Full field — strike rate ranked
Every batsman in one view, defaulting to strike rate. Hover for full stats. Change the metric to instantly re-rank.
Strike rate king
Rinku Patidar leads all 30 batsmen with a SR of 170.5 — 15 points clear of his nearest rival Klaasen (163.3). For context, he scores nearly 1.7 runs every ball he faces. In a competition that increasingly rewards boundary-hitters at the death, that number is extraordinary.
The SR vs average trade-off
Suryakumar Yadav (SR 156) and Nicholas Pooran (SR 156) sit just below Klaasen, but with lower averages than the elite group. They represent a clear finisher archetype — maximise output when set, accept the dismissal risk. AB de Villiers (SR 150, avg 37.7) remains the gold standard for combining both.
The slower end
Tiwary (SR 113), Uthappa (SR 117) and Pandey (SR 118) are at the bottom of the SR table — all below 120. In the modern IPL, these rates are increasingly hard to justify for specialist batsmen unless backed by exceptional averages, which none of them post.
